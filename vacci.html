Poultry Vaccination Schedules

Newcastle Disease

Dose 1

Administered to broiler and layer chicks within the first day of hatch. This initial vaccination is crucial for building immunity against Newcastle disease.

Dose 2

Follow-up vaccination given to broilers and layers approximately 2-3 weeks after the first dose. This booster shot enhances immunity and ensures long-term protection against Newcastle disease.

Bronchitis

Dose 1

Administered to broiler and layer chicks within the first week of age. This initial vaccination helps protect against infectious bronchitis and ensures respiratory health.

Dose 2

Secondary vaccination given to broilers and layers at around 4-6 weeks of age. This booster dose reinforces immunity against infectious bronchitis and reduces the risk of respiratory diseases.

Fowl Pox

Single Dose

Administered to broilers and layers at around 8-10 weeks of age. This vaccination provides immunity against fowl pox virus, preventing the development of clinical symptoms.

Marek's Disease

Single Dose

Administered to broilers and layers at one day old or as early as possible. Marek's disease vaccination is crucial for preventing tumors and paralysis caused by the Marek's disease virus.
